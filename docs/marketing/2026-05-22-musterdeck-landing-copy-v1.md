## Section B: What MusterDeck is

### Heading

**Use:**
> An operations console for Star Citizen crews

### Body

**Use:**
> MusterDeck is built by Star Citizen players, for Star Citizen players. It is not a Discord replacement, not a guild manager, and not affiliated with Cloud Imperium Games or Roberts Space Industries. It is a place to plan, run, and settle operations cleanly, then go back to flying.
>
> Four connected tools handle the parts that usually break down in chat: finding crew, planning ships and roles, settling loot and prizes, and running tournaments.

### Inline reassurance line (sits just below the body)

**Locked phrasing match:**
> Independent fan project. Not affiliated with Cloud Imperium Games, Roberts Space Industries, or their affiliates.

## Section C: The four pillars (cards open modal briefs)

### Card one-liners

Card 1, Rally Point: Find or publish operations.
Card 2, Fleet Command: Plan ships, staff crews, run ops.
Card 3, S.P.O.I.L.S.: Settle loot, payouts, and prize ledgers.
Card 4, Proving Ground: Run tournaments and competitive events.

### Modal brief: Rally Point

Title: Rally Point
Sub: Find or publish operations.

Body:
> Rally Point is the public board for Star Citizen ops. Hosts post what they're running, what roles they need, what ships they're bringing, and what kind of crew they want. Players browse, filter, and apply.
>
> You can browse Rally Point without an account. Posting an op or applying to one requires an account.
>
> For players looking for organized runs, and hosts who want more than a Discord callout.

CTA: Create account

### Modal brief: Fleet Command

Title: Fleet Command
Sub: Plan ships, staff crews, run ops.

Body:
> Fleet Command is for org officers planning real operations. It handles ship rosters, crew profiles, staffing templates, ship requests, roster locks, live ops views, and an audit log of who did what.
>
> It assumes your org is serious enough to want shared tools instead of a half-broken spreadsheet that only one person updates.
>
> For fleet admirals, officers, and crew leads running medium to large operations.

CTA: Create account

### Modal brief: S.P.O.I.L.S.

Title: S.P.O.I.L.S.
Sub: Settle loot, payouts, and prize ledgers.

Body:
> S.P.O.I.L.S. handles the part everyone agrees matters but nobody wants to do manually: settling what was earned, who gets what share, and what the org bank holds. It works for mining, salvage, cargo, ship components, ship weapons, sale proceeds, and tournament prizes.
>
> It is a record-keeper. It does not move real money, in-game currency, or in-game items. Transfers happen in-game between the players involved.
>
> For quartermasters, org leadership, and anyone who has lost a payout argument because nobody wrote it down.

CTA: Create account

### Modal brief: Proving Ground

Title: Proving Ground
Sub: Run tournaments and competitive events.

Body:
> Proving Ground runs tournaments. Single elimination, double elimination, round robin, Swiss, leaderboards, and group-stage-to-finals. 1v1 through 5v5 and custom formats. It covers signups, seeding, brackets, score entry, standings, and disputes.
>
> MusterDeck provides the tooling. Tournaments are organized and run by independent organizers, not by MusterDeck.
>
> For tournament organizers, competitive teams, racing leagues, and combat communities.

CTA: Create account

## Quality checks run on this draft

- No em dashes, no en dashes. Verified.
- No banned vocabulary. Verified.
- Active voice biased. Three passive constructions retained where active reads worse.
- "We" vs. "you" ratio. "We" appears in three places where the brand voice required it (the fan-project disclaimer, the cookie banner, the 404). "You" appears throughout the body copy. Ratio favors "you," consistent with the Rule of You.
- Oxford commas. Verified.
- Sentence case headers. Verified.
- No rule of three reflex. Four pillars produce four-item rhythms in the subhead and the body, which match the product structure honestly.
- No trailing summary at the bottom of any section.
- "Star Citizen" written out in full on first mention per section; not abbreviated to SC in body copy.
- Pillar names use exact capitalization including periods in S.P.O.I.L.S.
- Fan-project disclaimer reproduced verbatim from master plan.
