MusterDeck visual identity

A working language for the operations deck. Mustard caution paint on industrial dark base, stencil display type with mono utility, hazard contrast used as edge accent only. Four header treatment lanes at the bottom of this page. Pick one to push into production.

01 · Paint system

Color, committed

One mustard. Three layers under it.
Mustard #D4A028

The committed primary. Between Cat-yellow and Komatsu.

Sun-bleached #E3B651

Top-of-panel highlight. Reads as faded sun exposure.

Underlayer #B68A33

Visible at chip edges. Older paint underneath the topcoat.

Shadow #8A6818

Underside of panels. Deep recesses, grime collection.

Hazard black #0F0E0C

Stripes, stencil ink, contrast labels. Always on yellow.

Primer grey #6E6A60

First layer down from chipped paint. Never pure grey.

Bare metal #8A8682

Deepest chips only. Used sparingly, never as fill.

Panel deep #14130E

The page base. Warm-black, not blue-black.

Panel #1C1B16

Cards, sections, surfaces.

Panel raised #25241D

Inputs, raised tiles, secondary surfaces.

Rust accent #9C4A22

Warm accent. Sparing. Patches, edge wear, rare highlight.

Signal #B43A2A

Errors, alerts. Truly rare. Not a brand color.

02 · Textures

Material samples

Wear as seasoning, not the dish.

Worn paint

Mustard topcoat with three chip points showing faded underlayer rings. Directional scuffs run at the slight angle of motion across the surface.

Hazard tape · clean

Reference state. 135-degree black-on-yellow. Used at the edge of a band or panel, never as a full background fill.

Hazard tape · weathered

Same grammar with chip spots and sun fade. This is the production target for header-edge tape and active-tab accents.

MDOPS · STENCIL · 04

Stencil bridges

Real stencil cuts leave bridges in M, D, A, O, R, P. The display face must keep them visible. Faked stencil through a sliced sans face never reads right.

Primer wall

What sits under chipped paint. Warm grey with darker grime in low corners. Used for empty states and "no data" surfaces.

Patch grammar

Thick outer ring, sparse inner symbol, ring text in mono. Mission-patch logic for org badges, event marks, and the brand patch.

05 · Background

Naval chart, not topo blob

Sparse, gridded, labeled.
SECTOR-04 · LIVE PLOT
What this background is doing
  • Hairline grid at 48px on a 12px sub-grid. Reads as charted surface, never as wallpaper.
  • Two or three irregular contour arcs in faint mustard. No closed blobs.
  • Sparse waypoint markers with mono labels. Each pillar can map a waypoint to itself.
  • A dashed plotted route between two waypoints. Quiet sci-fi navigation cue.
  • Small contour labels in mono use mustard at low opacity so the background never competes for attention.

What it is not. Not the Vecteezy curvy-topo pattern, not generic dot-grid, not radial neon glow. Operational chart vocabulary, used quietly.

06 · Open decisions

What to lock next

After this page is reviewed

Lane choice. Pick one of the four header treatments. Each is a different commitment level to the mustard caution paint. The chosen lane sets the production direction for the header, then propagates to footer, hub cards, and pillar tiles.

Font asset. Stardos Stencil and Saira Condensed are free Google Fonts with open licenses, which removes the gitignore problem the current Capture It build has. Capture It can stay as a fallback if you prefer its texture, but recommendation is to ship the open-licensed pair to keep the repo clean.

Texture cost. The chip-through and worn-paint effects in this study are pure CSS and SVG, no raster textures. That keeps the bundle small and lets the same treatments work at any resolution. If we want stronger weathering, we can introduce a single optimized PNG noise overlay later.

Out of scope here. Pillar card visuals, footer treatment, empty states, button system, form fields. Those follow the chosen header lane.
