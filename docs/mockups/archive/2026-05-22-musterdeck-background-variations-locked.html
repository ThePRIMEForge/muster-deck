Backgrounds, by surface

All variants stay inside the same chart vocabulary: hairline grid, mustard waypoint accents, mono labels, dark warm-black base. What changes is the subject the chart is plotting. Space and orbits for the operational surfaces. Surface survey for the LFG board.

Variant A

Orbital Plot

Landing · Hub · Fleet Command
What this is: top-down view of a small system. Central star with halo, three concentric orbits, three planets at orbital positions with target reticles, one dashed slingshot trajectory crossing the system. Loudest of the four space variants.
Variant B

Course Plot

Landing · Hub · Fleet Command
What this is: angled orbits with one emphasized course plot. The path enters the chart on a dashed pre-burn, becomes solid for the transfer orbit, swings around a planet for a gravity assist, and exits toward the target. Reads as an active flight plan rather than a static map.
Variant C

Sector Chart

Landing · Hub · Fleet Command
What this is: tactical sector view. Stronger grid, three waypoints connected by solid and dashed routes, a hatched contested zone, distance and bearing call-outs. Reads as a current operational picture rather than astronomy.
Variant D

Jump Corridor

Landing · Hub · Fleet Command
What this is: wide system map. Four named systems sit at chart positions with halo glows, hairline jump corridors connect them with diamond jump-point markers at midpoints, and a compass rose anchors the orientation. Reads as a strategic overview rather than a single mission.
Variant E

Surface Survey

Rally Point · LFG board
What this is: planet-side surface map. Topographic contour lines with elevation labels, a dashed claim boundary marking a mining operation (AURUM-3), industrial symbol set (ore clusters, gem markers, pickaxes for work orders), a red hatched hostile zone with two attack reticles, three numbered friendly waypoints, supply-line dashes between them, and a map scale bar. Supports both industrial and combat LFG copy on the same canvas.
Variant F

Pyro System

Landing · Fleet Command · Wild ops
What this is: Pyro system in chart form. Red-giant central star with a wider, warmer halo. Broken asteroid debris rings instead of clean orbits, peppered with debris dots. Five named Pyro planets including Terminus with its contested Ruin Station. Three outlaw stations marked with diamond rests (Endgame, Checkmate, Rat's Nest). A "no UEE jurisdiction" warning runs along the bottom. Uses the rust accent #9C4A22 to mark Pyro as visibly different from the disciplined Stanton variants.
Variant G

Levski · Delamar

Asteroid base detail · Rally Point alt
What this is: close-up of Delamar with Levski cut into it. Irregular asteroid silhouette filled with a faint rocky stipple, a hex grid overlaid only on the asteroid surface, the city core circled at center with reticle, six labeled mining shafts hatched into the surface, dashed tunnel paths connecting shafts to the core, three docking ports on the rim, and a dashed approach vector to Dock A. Population stamp and rotation rate on the bottom edge. Reads as a working community inside a rock, not a planet.
Variant H

Asteroid Belt

Multi-base ops · S.P.O.I.L.S. · alt Rally
What this is: wide chart of an asteroid belt with multiple operating bases. A two-curve belt boundary with named bands runs diagonally across the canvas, salted with irregular asteroid polygons and a dense dot cloud. Three friendly stations (ARC-L1, HUR-L4, CRU-L5) sit on bigger host asteroids with reticles, plus one outlaw outpost in the upper right marked with the rust palette. Solid and dashed trade routes connect them, with a hatched mining claim straddling the belt. Reads as a busy regional trade and ops map, distinct from the system-scale views.
06 · Notes

Working notes

For decision

Family. All five variants stay on the same chart base (dark warm-black, hairline grid, mustard accents at low opacity, mono labels). The grammar changes; the toolkit doesn't.

How loud is a background? Each variant here is rendered at "preview brightness" so you can see what's in it. Production opacity should drop another 20-30 percent so content sits on top cleanly. The mustard waypoint accents should be the brightest thing on the canvas, and even those should sit under any active UI element.

What I'd flag.

  • Variants A and B are space-flight in vocabulary. A reads as a stable plot; B reads as an active course. Hub probably wants A (welcoming); Fleet Command wants B (operational).
  • Variant C is the most "tactical" of the space set. Could go on Fleet Command if you want it to feel busier, or stay reserved for specific dashboard surfaces.
  • Variant D works at landing and high-level surfaces where the strategic-overview feel adds to the welcome.
  • Variant E is the only variant using red signal color (for hostile zones and attack reticles). Worth deciding whether to keep that on the surface or pull it back to mustard-only with a different mark for hostile.
  • Real space backgrounds in apps need to be careful with stars over text. We can keep all of these but tile them with stronger top/bottom fades when implemented, so star density drops in the header and footer zones where text lives.
