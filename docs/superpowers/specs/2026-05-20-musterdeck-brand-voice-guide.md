# MusterDeck Brand Voice Guide

Date: 2026-05-20

## Purpose

This guide defines how MusterDeck speaks across public pages, app screens, notifications, admin controls, Rally Point listings, Fleet Command operations, and S.P.O.I.L.S. settlements.

The visual brand guide will be finalized separately. Current visual direction is mustard yellow, drab green, vintage military utility, worn material texture, and modern operational clarity. This document focuses only on language.

## Voice Position

MusterDeck sounds like an organized operations desk for Star Citizen players.

It should feel:

- Pragmatic.
- Concise.
- Operational.
- Inviting.
- Disciplined.
- Grounded in Star Citizen language.
- Useful for both combat and non-combat game loops.

The voice should help players feel like they are joining a coordinated crew, not reading marketing copy.

## Core Voice Formula

Operational command, light conscription, broad crew respect.

Meaning:

- Operational command: the product gives clear orders, statuses, labels, and next actions.
- Light conscription: the product can invite people to report in, join the ranks, muster, and take a station.
- Broad crew respect: miners, salvagers, haulers, medics, engineers, scouts, quartermasters, escorts, pilots, and FPS players all belong on the deck.

## Voice Pillars

### 1. Clear Orders

Copy should make the next action obvious.

Good:

- Report in.
- Join operation.
- Claim station.
- Save event.
- Lock roster.
- Settle shares.

Avoid:

- Begin your incredible journey.
- Unlock the ultimate experience.
- Dominate the stars.

### 2. Organized Crew

MusterDeck should frame participation as coordinated, not chaotic.

Good:

- Keep the roster clean.
- Assign the crew before launch.
- Bring the right ship to the right job.
- Every role has a station.

Avoid:

- Everyone pile in.
- Anything goes.
- Chaos is the plan.

### 3. In-The-Verse, Not Official

MusterDeck can use Star Citizen community language, but it must not sound like an official RSI/CIG system.

Use carefully:

- the 'verse
- citizen
- mobiGlas
- Comm-Link
- Spectrum
- aUEC
- UEE
- Stanton
- Pyro
- cargo grid
- SCU
- mission
- contract
- org

Avoid:

- Official Fleet Command
- RSI-approved
- CIG-backed
- guaranteed payout
- verified by Cloud Imperium
- official UEE orders

### 4. Respect The Whole Crew

The app should not sound like it only serves combat players.

Combat players can receive sharper command language:

- Enlist.
- Engage.
- Extract.
- Hold the line.
- Assign fireteams.

Industrial and logistics players should receive steadier, more professional language:

- Coordinate the haul.
- Staff the run.
- Track the load.
- Keep cargo moving.
- Log the yield.
- Settle the shares.

Support players should be framed as essential:

- Keep the fleet supplied.
- Cover the route.
- Maintain the line.
- Recover the crew.
- Repair, refuel, rearm.

## Tone By Context

### Public Landing

Tone: confident, inviting, broad.

Goal: explain that MusterDeck supports the full operation lifecycle without over-indexing on combat.

Use:

- Rally the crew.
- Build the roster.
- Run the operation.
- Settle the rewards.
- Across the 'verse.

Avoid:

- War-only positioning.
- Aggressive recruitment language.
- Overly cinematic hero copy.

Sample:

MusterDeck helps citizens rally crews, command operations, and settle rewards across the 'verse.

### Rally Point

Tone: open, practical, match-making oriented.

Goal: help players find operations by role, ship, activity, and trust requirement.

Use:

- Find your next run.
- Join a crew.
- Offer a ship.
- Apply for a station.
- Browse open operations.
- Looking for miners.
- Escort needed.
- Cargo crew forming.

Avoid:

- Casual LFG chaos.
- Combat-only phrasing.
- Promising guaranteed payout or success.

Sample:

Find an operation that needs your ship, your role, or your steady hands on the job.

### Fleet Command

Tone: command-forward, concise, high-clarity.

Goal: help officers plan and run operations without ambiguity.

Use:

- Set event orders.
- Build fleet line.
- Assign crew.
- Lock ship roster.
- Move to team.
- Confirm station.
- Request change.

Combat examples:

- Strike group forming.
- Fireteam assigned.
- Extract phase active.

Industrial examples:

- Haul route forming.
- Salvage crew assigned.
- Mining wing staffed.
- Support element ready.

Avoid:

- Fake rank shouting.
- Long tactical flavor text inside workflows.
- Labels that only make sense for combat.

### S.P.O.I.L.S.

Tone: fair, accountable, quartermaster-led.

Goal: make settlement feel orderly and trustworthy.

Use:

- Log the haul.
- Record proceeds.
- Approve claim.
- Settle shares.
- Reserve org bank.
- Finalize payout.
- Mark paid.

Avoid:

- Winner takes all.
- Guaranteed reward.
- Loot drama language.
- Anything that sounds like real-money value.

Sample:

Log the haul, approve the claims, and settle the shares.

### Admin Portal

Tone: restrained, precise, procedural.

Goal: maintain order without sounding punitive by default.

Use:

- Review account.
- Restrict access.
- Restore access.
- Require approval.
- Add moderation note.
- Account state.

Avoid:

- Punish.
- Blacklist.
- Exile.
- Enemy.
- Threat.

Sample:

Maintain order across the deck.

### Account And Profile

Tone: plain, trust-building, low-friction.

Goal: help users complete identity setup without making the product feel bureaucratic.

Use:

- Callsign.
- Link Discord.
- Verify RSI handle.
- Set notification preferences.
- Complete profile.

Avoid:

- Mandatory roleplay identity.
- Unclear trust language.
- Overstating what verification proves.

Sample:

Complete your profile so hosts know who is reporting in.

### Notifications

Tone: short, action-focused, neutral.

Goal: tell users what changed and what to do next.

Use:

- Assignment updated.
- Role invite received.
- Ship offer approved.
- Roster locked.
- Settlement finalized.
- Payout marked paid.

Avoid:

- Dramatic alert copy for normal events.
- Jokes.
- Long explanations in notification titles.

## Copy Patterns

### Primary CTA Patterns

- Create account
- Report in
- Join operation
- Create operation
- Build roster
- Save event
- Publish listing
- Start settlement
- Finalize payout

### Secondary CTA Patterns

- Browse listings
- View details
- Request change
- Save draft
- Copy join link
- Link Discord
- Verify RSI handle
- Review queue

### Empty State Patterns

Use short statements with a clear action.

Examples:

- No open operations. Create one or check Rally Point later.
- No crew assigned. Start with pilots, then fill support roles.
- No pending approvals. The queue is clear.
- No settlement items. Log the haul when the operation wraps.
- No notifications. Orders will appear here.

### Error Patterns

Errors should state what failed and what the user can try next.

Examples:

- Assignment failed. Check access and try again.
- Event could not be saved. Review required fields.
- Join request blocked. This operation requires RSI verification.
- Payout could not be finalized. Confirm all shares first.

### Success Patterns

Success messages should confirm the result without celebration overload.

Examples:

- Event saved.
- Roster locked.
- Assignment updated.
- Ship offer approved.
- Settlement finalized.
- Profile updated.

## Do / Don't

Do:

- Use short command-style labels.
- Respect non-combat roles as first-class participants.
- Use Star Citizen vocabulary where it helps orientation.
- Keep trust and verification language precise.
- Use "crew" more often than "soldiers."
- Use "operation" as the broad umbrella term.

Don't:

- Make every flow sound like combat.
- Use real-world military severity for routine UI.
- Overuse "the 'verse" in every paragraph.
- Make payouts sound guaranteed.
- Sound official, CIG-backed, or RSI-integrated.
- Copy CrewTerminal, scfleets.space, RSI, or Star Citizen Wiki wording directly.

## Legal And Trust Boundaries

MusterDeck must always remain clearly fan-made and independent.

Required posture:

- Star Citizen terms may be referenced as community/game context.
- RSI/CIG ownership must be acknowledged in legal/footer copy.
- No page should imply official endorsement.
- RSI handle verification should be described as a trust signal, not proof of identity outside the game.
- S.P.O.I.L.S. values and payouts should be framed as organizer-managed records, not guarantees.

## Source Vocabulary Notes

The voice guide is informed by public Star Citizen/RSI language patterns, including:

- RSI Support uses "the 'verse" and "citizen" in new player and daily-life guidance.
- RSI Comm-Link uses short event headlines and mission-style copy such as "Enlist. Engage. Extract."
- RSI Support describes aUEC as Alpha United Earth Credits and emphasizes that it is temporary alpha currency.
- RSI Support and Comm-Link language around mining, commodities, cargo, salvage, mobiGlas, and missions reinforces the need for industrial and logistics vocabulary alongside combat language.

## Open Decisions

These should be finalized after visual identity and iconography are locked:

- Whether "Report in" is the main account/signup CTA or reserved for app actions.
- Whether "Operations Hub" or "Command Deck" is the logged-in home label.
- Whether public copy should say "Star Citizen operations" or "operations across the 'verse" first.
- Whether "member" or "citizen" should be preferred in account/profile copy.
- Whether industrial activity pages should say "runs," "operations," or "contracts" by default.

## Recommended Default

Use "operation" as the universal unit.

Use "crew" as the universal participant group.

Use "station" as the universal role/position label when a role can be combat, ship, logistics, medical, salvage, mining, or cargo.

Use "the 'verse" as flavor, not filler.

Use softer industrial/logistics language whenever the activity is not explicitly combat.
